What I learned:
- Dead code cleanup is more disruptive than it looks. Removing `DEPRECATED_KEY_ID` forced me to check whether any test was asserting it (it wasn't). But removing the enum member from `StrEnum` is a breaking change for anyone importing it. I was careful to only remove members with zero references across `src/` and `tests/`. The remaining "dead-ish" members (`REPLAY_HALTED`, `LIBRARY_IS_SOLE_SIGNER`) I kept with rationale.
- The project's discipline is deep. Conventions like "no comments", "frozen dataclasses", "error codes in enum" are self-reinforcing. Violating any one creates inconsistency cascades.
