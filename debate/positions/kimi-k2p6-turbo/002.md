# Position on Substrate Debate 002 — Workflow composition

**Defer until Phase 4 YAML is drafted and measured. Add linting thresholds now as the trigger mechanism.**

## Why the problem is real but unmeasured

The debate estimates Phase 4 YAML at 200+ lines based on adding jury gates, race patterns, and cross-family reviewers. That's a forecast, not data. `phase2.yaml` is ~80 lines and well-structured. The debate's own proposed solution includes an excellent measurement step: "draft `phase4.yaml` on paper and if it exceeds 150 lines, implement `include`." I agree with that step — but it hasn't happened yet.

## Why linting thresholds are the right immediate step

A `make lint-workflow` target that fails if a workflow YAML exceeds 150 lines or 5 nesting levels:
- Costs ~30 lines of Python
- No schema changes
- No runtime impact
- Makes the pain visible without solving it preemptively

If Phase 4 YAML drafts exceed the threshold, the lint failure is the signal to build `include`. If it doesn't, the lint passes and no composition infrastructure was needed.

This is the same pattern as Debate 010 (event log retention): instrument first, build when threshold fires.

## Why `include` is the right design but wrong timing

The debate's proposed `include:` list with last-wins semantics is correct. But building it now (Phase 2) means:
- Maintaining `include` parsing, circular detection, and composition validation through Phases 3 and 4
- No actual multi-file workflows to test it against
- Risk of fragment boundaries that don't align with Phase 4's natural decomposition

## What I'd push back on

The debate's framing that "the principal cannot review a 200-line YAML for correctness" implies composition is the solution. But composition trades one review problem (long file) for another (cross-file consistency). The actual solution is machine validation (`validate_yaml` already exists, BC-061). A 200-line YAML that passes `validate_yaml` is more trustworthy than 6 × 40-line fragments that the validator checks individually but not for cross-file consistency.

## Concrete next step

1. Add `lint-workflow` target to Makefile (150-line threshold, 5 nesting levels).
2. When Phase 4 planning begins, draft `phase4.yaml` inline.
3. If draft exceeds threshold, implement `include` primitive.
4. Not before.
